Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy explains how FormGuard by WP Expert (“FormGuard”, “we”, “us”, or “our”) collects, uses, discloses, and protects personal information when you visit our website, interact with our content, or use our services. We are committed to respecting your privacy and handling your data in a transparent and secure way.

Last updated: November 26, 2025

1. Introduction

This Privacy Policy applies to the FormGuard marketing site, related landing pages, and any communication channels where this policy is referenced (collectively, the “Services”). By accessing or using our Services, you acknowledge that you have read and understood this Privacy Policy.

We aim to collect only the information we need in order to:

  • Provide and improve our Services.
  • Communicate with prospects, clients, and partners.
  • Understand how our website is being used.
  • Maintain the security, reliability, and performance of our systems.

If you do not agree with this policy, you should discontinue use of our Services.

2. Information We Collect

We collect information in three main ways: (a) information you provide directly, (b) information collected automatically, and (c) information from third parties or integrations.

2.1 Information You Provide to Us

We collect information that you voluntarily provide when you interact with our Services, such as when you:

  • Fill out a contact, quote, or inquiry form.
  • Request information, demos, or support.
  • Subscribe to newsletters or marketing communications.
  • Respond to surveys, questionnaires, or feedback requests.

The types of information you may provide include:

  • Name, company name, and job title.
  • Contact details such as email address and phone number.
  • Website URLs and technical details relevant to your WordPress setup.
  • Any other information you choose to include in messages or attachments.

2.2 Information Collected Automatically

When you visit our website, certain information is collected automatically to help us understand usage patterns and maintain site security and performance. This may include:

  • IP address and approximate location (country or region).
  • Browser type, device type, and operating system.
  • Pages viewed, referring URLs, and timestamps of visits.
  • Interactions with our site such as clicks, scrolls, and form views.

This information is typically collected through cookies, analytics tools, and server logs. We use it in aggregated or pseudonymized form wherever possible.

2.3 Information from Third Parties and Integrations

If you contact us through a third-party platform (such as a form service, analytics provider, or communication tool), we may receive certain information about you from those services in accordance with their own privacy policies. We use this information solely for the purposes described in this Privacy Policy.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for the following purposes:

  • Providing and maintaining our Services: To respond to your inquiries, schedule calls, prepare proposals, and deliver the FormGuard service where applicable.
  • Communication: To send you service-related messages, respond to support requests, and provide important updates related to your use of our Services.
  • Improvement and analytics: To understand how our website and content are used, diagnose technical issues, and optimize performance and usability.
  • Marketing (where permitted): To send you relevant information about FormGuard, WP Expert, new features, or related services you may find valuable. You can opt out of marketing communications at any time.
  • Security and fraud prevention: To protect our Services, investigate suspicious activity, and help prevent malicious or unauthorized use.
  • Legal compliance: To comply with applicable laws, regulations, and legal processes, and to enforce our agreements and policies.

We do not sell your personal information. We only use and share it as outlined in this Privacy Policy.

4. Cookies and Similar Technologies

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit a website. Like many websites, we may use cookies and similar technologies to:

  • Remember your preferences and improve your browsing experience.
  • Understand how visitors navigate our pages and which content is most useful.
  • Monitor performance, security, and usage trends of our website.

4.1 Types of Cookies We May Use

  • Strictly necessary cookies: Required for basic website functionality and security. These cannot typically be disabled via cookie banners.
  • Performance and analytics cookies: Help us understand how visitors use our site so we can improve layout, content, and navigation.
  • Functional cookies: Allow the site to remember choices you make (such as preferred language) and provide enhanced features.

4.2 Managing Cookies

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings, including blocking or deleting them. Please note that disabling certain cookies may impact the functionality or performance of our website.

For more information on managing cookies, consult your browser’s help documentation or visit widely available resources on cookie management tools.

5. Data Sharing and Disclosure

We may share your information with third parties in the limited circumstances described below:

  • Service providers: We may engage trusted third-party vendors (such as hosting providers, email delivery services, analytics platforms, and form processors) to help operate our Services. These providers are granted access only to the information necessary to perform their functions and are contractually obligated to protect it.
  • Business transfers: In the event of a merger, acquisition, reorganization, or asset sale, your information may be transferred as part of that transaction, subject to continued protection consistent with this Privacy Policy.
  • Legal requirements and protection: We may disclose information if required to do so by law or in the good-faith belief that such action is necessary to comply with legal obligations, protect our rights or property, or protect the safety of users or the public.

We do not sell personal information to third parties and do not share it for their independent marketing purposes.

6. Data Retention

We retain personal information for as long as reasonably necessary to fulfill the purposes described in this Privacy Policy, including:

  • To respond to your inquiries and maintain a business relationship.
  • To comply with legal, accounting, or reporting obligations.
  • To resolve disputes and enforce our agreements.

When information is no longer needed for these purposes, we will delete it or anonymize it in accordance with applicable laws and our internal data retention practices.

7. Data Security

We take reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect personal information from unauthorized access, loss, misuse, or alteration. These measures may include secure hosting environments, access controls, encryption in transit, and other safeguards aligned with industry practices.

However, no method of transmission over the internet or electronic storage is completely secure. While we strive to protect your information, we cannot guarantee absolute security. You are encouraged to use caution when sharing sensitive information online.

Depending on your location and applicable law, you may have certain rights regarding your personal information, which can include:

  • The right to request correction or update of inaccurate or incomplete data.
